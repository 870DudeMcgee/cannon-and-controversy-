# Site Visual Language Notes

This note translates the current website into collection criteria for the documentary library.

## Existing Motifs On The Site

- Codex hero treatment
- Ethiopian manuscript inspired miniature layer
- Scroll-fragment card logic
- Gold rule lines and ornamental dividers
- Red and green manuscript accent textures over dark archival backgrounds
- Timeline tables and fact bars
- Scholarly typography with a ceremonial tone

## What To Pull From The Site

Capture or recreate these into `site-derived/` folders:

- parchment backgrounds and tonal gradients
- codex silhouette references
- divider geometry
- section plate framing ideas
- label hierarchy for dates, book numbers, and callouts
- color references for gold, dim gold, text-ivory, background-charcoal, and manuscript reds

## What To Match Externally

When collecting outside imagery, prefer visuals that feel like they could live beside the site without aesthetic friction:

- matte surfaces over glossy ones
- aged gold over shiny gold
- textured paper over clean digital white
- candlelit or low-key atmosphere over bright studio light
- manuscript reds and mineral greens used as accents, not dominant floods
- frontal or symmetrical compositions for authority
- macro details for tactility
