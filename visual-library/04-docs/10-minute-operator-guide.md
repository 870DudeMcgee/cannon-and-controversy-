# 10-Minute Operator Guide

This guide is for returning to the visual library without getting buried in everything at once.

## The Only Mental Model You Need

- The boards show the look.
- The core collections hold historical source material.
- The series kits hold reusable graphics and design parts.
- The docs tell you what to collect and how to organize it.
- The tracker proves that an asset is real, sourced, and usable.

## Start Here Every Time

1. Open `visual-library/index.html`.
2. Open `visual-library/folder-map.html` if you feel disoriented.
3. Choose one lane only:
   - overall look -> `style-board.html`
   - geography -> `map-board.html`
   - quotations and source cards -> `quote-source-board.html`
4. Collect or review only one category in one session.
5. Log what you touched in `asset-tracker.csv`.

## What Each Surface Is For

### `index.html`
Your hub. Use this to move between boards, manifests, sourcing docs, and tracker files.

### `folder-map.html`
Your orientation page. Use this when you forget what the folders are for or where a new asset belongs.

### `style-board.html`
Use this to judge tone. Ask: does the new asset feel like it belongs in this world?

### `map-board.html`
Use this for Jerusalem, Rome, diaspora, routes, conflict geography, and locator systems.

### `quote-source-board.html`
Use this when you need witness cards, citations, quotations, thesis plates, or author/source intros.

## What To Ignore For Now

Ignore anything that feels like "later production polish" until you have a reason to use it.

You do not need to memorize:
- every pack manifest
- every SVG asset
- every sourcing batch
- every category at once

You only need to know where to start and where to put the next useful thing.

## The Safe Working Routine

1. Decide what you are working on.
2. Open the matching board.
3. Pull a small batch of source material.
4. Drop it into the correct collection folder.
5. Rename or log it before moving on.
6. Promote only the strongest items into `curated/`.

## The Three Questions To Ask Before Keeping Anything

1. Does it fit one of the recurring motifs?
2. Can it be reused beyond one episode?
3. Is the source and license clear enough to log immediately?

If the answer to any of those is no, do not promote it yet.

## Best First Actions When You Return

Pick one of these, not all three:

- review the boards for 5 minutes
- collect 5 map assets
- collect 5 manuscript assets
- clean and rename one small raw batch
- turn one pending tracker row into a real sourced row

## The Main Mistake To Avoid

Do not browse aimlessly.

Open the hub, choose one lane, work one small batch, log it, stop.

That is enough progress.
