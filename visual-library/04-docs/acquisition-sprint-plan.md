# Acquisition Sprint Plan

This is the system for filling the library aggressively without losing control.

## Weekly Rhythm

### Sprint 1: Site-Derived Extraction

Goal:
- build immediate continuity with the current website

Collect or recreate:
- codex silhouettes
- parchment fields
- gold divider rules
- red accent fields
- Ethiopian miniature references
- timeline marker styles

Target:
- 40 reusable assets

### Sprint 2: Manuscripts and Scroll Surfaces

Goal:
- build the base texture language for the whole series

Target:
- 60 raw assets
- 20 curated assets
- 10 overlays

### Sprint 3: Maps and Jerusalem Geography

Goal:
- secure the navigation language for history sequences

Target:
- 50 raw assets
- 20 curated assets
- 5 map templates for reuse

### Sprint 4: Ethiopian Art and Typography

Goal:
- lock the series ornament system and title hierarchy

Target:
- 60 raw assets
- 25 curated assets
- 12 border or initial references

### Sprint 5: Roman Empire and Ancient Ruins

Goal:
- build the imperial and collapse vocabulary

Target:
- 70 raw assets
- 25 curated assets

### Sprint 6: Paintings and Church Interiors

Goal:
- gather emotional and institutional scale imagery

Target:
- 70 raw assets
- 25 curated assets

### Sprint 7: Candles and Atmospheric Inserts

Goal:
- fill transitional and contemplative gaps

Target:
- 40 raw assets
- 15 curated assets
- 8 overlay candidates

## Review Threshold

Do not move assets to `curated/` unless they score well in at least three areas:

- texture richness
- thematic fit
- motif usefulness
- compositing flexibility
- license clarity

## Daily Intake Rules

- Pull broadly in the morning.
- Rename and tag after each hunt session.
- Promote only the strongest 20 to 30 percent.
- Build derivatives the same day while the asset logic is still fresh.
- Update `asset-tracker.csv` before closing the session.

## Obsessive Collection Without Chaos

Use this ratio:

- 60 percent foundational reusable materials
- 25 percent historical specificity
- 15 percent beauty and atmosphere assets

That balance prevents the library from becoming all references and no working parts.

## Deliverables By The Time Episode Building Starts

You want these ready before assembling episodes:

- one stable parchment background pack
- one stable illuminated border pack
- one stable red and gold title system
- one stable map annotation system
- one stable codex-opening move
- one stable timeline package

Those are the core of the cinematic vocabulary.
