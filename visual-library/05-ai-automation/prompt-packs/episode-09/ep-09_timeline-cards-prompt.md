# Episode 09 Timeline Cards Prompt Pack

Generate concise documentary-series text in a scholarly but cinematic tone.

Task goal:
Generate timeline milestone cards, witness cards, chronology strips, and end-card lines for Episode 09.

Target use:
timeline cards, evidence strips, lower thirds, witness cards, end-cards

Text inputs:
- early formation:
  - Palestinian canon begins to close
  - Septuagint created in Alexandria
  - Yavneh or Jamnia process as later rabbinic consolidation after 70 AD
- early Christian witnesses:
  - Muratorian Fragment
  - Irenaeus defends the larger canon
  - Origen lists narrowly but uses widely
  - Athanasius distinguishes primary canon and edification tier
- formal Christian canon decisions:
  - Synod of Rome
  - Hippo and Carthage
  - Innocent I confirmation
  - Jerome and the Vulgate
  - Florence
- Reformation and closure:
  - Leipzig debate and Luther's 1534 Apocrypha section
  - Trent defines the Catholic canon
  - Westminster formally excludes the books
  - British and Foreign Bible Society stops printing the Apocrypha in low-cost Bibles
- framing lines:
  - each decision left a permanent mark on what Christians read as scripture
  - the canon was shaped across centuries
  - the books vanished from common Protestant awareness long after Luther

Style rules:
- concise, sober, and legible on screen
- emphasize chronology and consequence
- suitable for milestone cards and timeline strips
- distinguish witnesses from formal decisions

Generate:
- 10 milestone card headings or lines for the major canon events
- 6 witness-card lines for Irenaeus, Origen, Athanasius, Jerome, Luther, and Westminster
- 4 chronology-strip sets from Septuagint to Bible Society cutoff
- 4 short finale end-card lines

Must avoid:
- sensationalism
- collapsing all stages into one event
- excessive jargon
- excessive length
