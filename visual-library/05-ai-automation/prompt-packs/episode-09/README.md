# Episode 09 Prompt Packs

Episode 09 closes the series by turning the canon debate into a chronology viewers can hold in their heads: from the Septuagint and Yavneh-era consolidation through Hippo, Jerome, Luther, Trent, Westminster, and the nineteenth-century disappearance of the books from common Protestant print culture.

Use these packs to populate:
- opener and chapter-title variants
- timeline cards for major canon milestones
- witness and decision cards for Yavneh, Irenaeus, Origen, Athanasius, Rome, Hippo, Jerome, Luther, Trent, Westminster, and the Bible Society cutoff
- end-cards for the claim that the canon was shaped across centuries rather than dropped into history all at once

This episode should feel like the series finale. The governing argument is that the canon was formed by a long chain of scholarly, rabbinic, ecclesial, translational, and reforming decisions that permanently shaped what later Christians read as scripture.
