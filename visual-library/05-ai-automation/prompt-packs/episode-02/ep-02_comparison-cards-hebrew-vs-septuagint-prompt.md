# Episode 02 Comparison Cards Prompt Pack

Generate concise documentary-series text in a scholarly but cinematic tone.

Task goal:
Generate comparison-card copy, source-caption variants, and short on-screen evidence strips for Episode 02.

Target use:
comparison cards, source strips, evidence cards, lower thirds

Text inputs:
- comparison side A:
  - Hebrew Bible / TaNaKh
  - Palestine
  - Hebrew and Aramaic
  - Torah, Nebiim, Kethubim
  - Josephus counts 22 books around 95 AD
  - Protestant Old Testament follows this collection in content
- comparison side B:
  - Septuagint / LXX
  - Alexandria, Egypt
  - Greek
  - translation begun around 250 BC
  - includes the Hebrew Bible plus 7 additional books and expanded sections
  - early Christians used this larger collection
- evidence lines:
  - New Testament writers quote the Old Testament roughly 350 times
  - roughly 300 of those quotations favor Septuagint language over the Hebrew text
  - the books Protestants call Apocrypha are the same books Catholics call Deuterocanonical
  - these books were removed from the Protestant Bible during the Reformation rather than added to the Catholic Bible

Style rules:
- concise, sober, and confident
- readable on screen
- comparative rather than argumentative in the first half
- firmer in the final removal-not-addition section

Generate:
- 4 comparison-card variants for Hebrew Bible vs Septuagint
- 4 short evidence strips for Josephus, the Septuagint, and New Testament quotation patterns
- 4 final-card variants for the line removed, not added

Must avoid:
- sensationalism
- doctrinal preaching
- excessive jargon
- unsupported precision beyond the supplied lines
