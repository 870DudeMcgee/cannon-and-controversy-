# Episode 03 Quote and Source Prompt Pack

Generate concise documentary-series text in a scholarly but cinematic tone.

Task goal:
Generate quote-card copy, source strips, and lower-third variants for Episode 03.

Target use:
quote cards, source strips, lower thirds, evidence cards

Text inputs:
- primary figures:
  - Martin Luther
  - Johann Eck
  - James
  - Augustine
  - Jerome
  - Westminster Assembly
- primary source lines:
  - "Apocrypha: these are books which are not held equal to the Holy Scriptures, and yet are profitable and good to read."
  - Luther called James "an epistle of straw"
- evidence beats:
  - 2 Maccabees 12:44-46 was used in the Leipzig Debate of 1519
  - Luther denied the book's scriptural status rather than contesting the verse's meaning
  - Luther's canon criteria: Hebrew Bible, proclaim Christ, not contradict Pauline grace
  - the first criterion removes the deuterocanonical books
  - the third criterion nearly removes James
  - later Protestant confessions hardened the line beyond Luther

Style rules:
- concise, sober, and confident
- readable on screen
- suitable for witness cards and source strips
- emphasize doctrinal conflict as the driver of the cut

Generate:
- 4 variants each for Luther, Johann Eck, and James-related evidence strips
- 4 quote-card variants for the Luther Apocrypha preface line
- 4 short end-card lines for the Westminster hardening beat

Must avoid:
- caricature or mockery
- modern polemical tone
- unsupported claims
- excessive length
