# Episode 01 Quote Card Prompt Pack

Generate concise documentary-series text in a scholarly but cinematic tone.

Task goal:
Generate quote-card and source-caption variants for Episode 01 using two core witness lines already present in the site: the Caiaphas line from John 11 and the Josephus line about Jesus and the Christians.

Target use:
quote cards and source-caption cards

Text inputs:
- primary text A:
  - "You do not understand that it is better for you to have one man die for the people than to have the whole nation destroyed."
- secondary text A:
  - Caiaphas, high priestly deliberation before Jesus's arrest
- citations A:
  - John 11:49–50
- primary text B:
  - "There was also about this time Jesus, a wise man... And when Pilate, at the suggestion of the principal men amongst us, had condemned him to the cross, those that loved him at the first did not forsake him... And the tribe of Christians, so named from him, are not extinct to this day."
- secondary text B:
  - Josephus on Jesus and the Christians
- citations B:
  - Antiquities XVIII.3.3
- dates:
  - first century
  - ~93–95 AD for Josephus as source context
- names:
  - Caiaphas
  - Josephus
  - Pilate
  - Jesus

Style rules:
- concise, sober, and confident
- no clickbait language
- no exaggerated certainty
- readable on screen
- suitable for quote cards, chapter titles, lower thirds, or source captions

Generate:
- 3 variants for the Caiaphas card
- 3 variants for the Josephus card
- for each, provide one safest scholarly version, one slightly more dramatic but still credible version, and one shortest on-screen version

Must avoid:
- sensationalism
- modern marketing tone
- unsupported claims
- excessive length

Keep these rules:
- preserve the political rather than sentimental force of the Caiaphas line
- keep Josephus framed as a historical witness card, not as absolute proof text
- preserve source attribution clarity over drama
