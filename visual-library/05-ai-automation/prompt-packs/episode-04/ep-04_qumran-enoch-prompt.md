# Episode 04 Quote and Evidence Prompt Pack

Generate concise documentary-series text in a scholarly but cinematic tone.

Task goal:
Generate quote-card copy, source strips, lower thirds, and evidence-card variants for Episode 04.

Target use:
quote cards, evidence strips, lower thirds, comparison cards

Text inputs:
- primary subjects:
  - Qumran
  - Dead Sea Scrolls
  - Cave 4
  - Cave 1
  - Cave 11
  - 1 Enoch
  - Jubilees
  - Ethiopian Orthodox Church
- primary evidence beats:
  - Qumran falls around 68 AD before Jerusalem's destruction in 70 AD
  - Cave 4 holds roughly 90 percent of the fragments in chaotic deposit
  - Caves 1 and 11 preserve jar-sealed manuscripts in deliberate storage
  - the dominant scholarly model treats the caves as disaster-recovery storage
  - 1 Enoch appears in 11 Aramaic fragments dated roughly 200 BC to 1 BC
  - Enoch was ancient, heavily copied, and preserved long before Christianity became institutionalized
  - Enoch remains canonical in the Ethiopian Orthodox Church today
- framing lines:
  - exclusion did not mean non-circulation
  - the canon story is about a collapsing textual ecosystem, not one clean list

Style rules:
- concise, sober, and confident
- readable on screen
- suitable for evidence cards and witness strips
- emphasize material survival and textual authority under pressure

Generate:
- 4 variants each for Qumran, Cave 4 and Cave 11 evidence strips
- 4 quote-card or evidence-card variants for 1 Enoch's importance
- 4 short end-card lines for the survival-versus-exclusion conclusion

Must avoid:
- sensational archaeology tone
- unsupported certainty
- excessive jargon
- excessive length
