Orientation Surface

How the visual library is laid out.

This page is not for production. It is just a map so you can tell what each folder does, what order to use it in, and where a new asset belongs when you find one.

If you ever feel lost, come here first, then return to the command center once you know which lane you are in.

The simple model
  • Boards show the look.
  • Core collections store the historical material.
  • Series kits store the reusable graphics.
  • Docs tell you what to collect and log.
  • Ingest is the temporary landing area.

Working Order

1

Orient

Use the boards and motif docs to decide what kind of visual you need.

  • Style board for overall tone
  • Map board for geography
  • Quote board for citations and witness cards
2

Collect

Pull source material into the right category under core collections.

  • Start with raw
  • Favor public-domain sources
  • Collect narrowly, not everything at once
3

Log

Record the source, license, and intended use in the tracker as you go.

  • Do not postpone metadata
  • One row per real asset
  • Keep the name pattern consistent
4

Promote

Move the best assets from raw into curated or supporting overlays and cutouts.

  • Curated means approved
  • Cutouts are isolated elements
  • Overlays are atmosphere layers
5

Reuse

Use the series kits when you need title cards, borders, markers, chapter plates, and source cards.

  • These are reusable by design
  • Do not duplicate them per episode
  • Keep them episode-neutral unless needed

00-signature-motifs

The recurring moves that make the series feel consistent over time. This is identity, not storage.

01-core-collections

The historical archive itself. This is where you store collected source imagery by category.

02-series-kits

The reusable design tools: borders, title cards, quote windows, chapter plates, markers, and overlays.

03-ingest

The short-term holding zone for new finds before they are renamed and moved to the right category.

04-docs

The manuals: what to collect, where to look, how to name it, and how to track it.

05-ai-automation

The handoff layer for other AI tools: structured briefs, reusable prompts, and run logs.

Boards

The visual reference surfaces that help you judge whether new material actually belongs in this world.

Three Rules That Prevent Chaos

Rule One

Collect in small themed batches. Do not open five categories at once.

Rule Two

If it is reusable across episodes, it belongs in the kits or motif system, not buried inside an episode folder.

Rule Three

If it is not logged or renamed, it is not really part of the library yet.
