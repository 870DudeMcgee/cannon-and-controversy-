# 02 Production: Episode 07

- episode title: Why It Matters
- episode code: ep-07_why-it-matters
- runtime target: 9 to 14 minutes for the first documentary cut
- governing argument: The canon debate is not merely academic. It changed what readers can see directly in scripture about prayer for the dead, immortality, evil, intertestamental history, and the New Testament's own literary world.
- tone priority: culmination and clarity first, controversy second

## Core Thesis

This episode should argue that canon decisions shaped real doctrine outcomes, altered how believers imagine the afterlife and evil, erased key historical context for Jesus and the apostles, and obscured the New Testament's source world.

It should function as the direct sequel to Episode 06: once viewers see that doctrine reorganizes around canon choices, Episode 07 shows why that reorganization matters to ordinary readers and to the historical understanding of the Bible itself.

## Suggested Shot Order

1. Open on the claim that the canon question is not merely academic.
2. Name Hippo Trent and Westminster as decisions with downstream consequences.
3. State that what follows is about changed belief, changed reading, and changed historical memory.
4. Chapter break into doctrinal stakes.
5. Present 2 Maccabees 12 as the clearest scriptural support for prayers for the dead.
6. Present Wisdom 3 as the clearest direct immortality text.
7. State that removing these texts does not remove the questions; it changes the terms on which they are answered.
8. Chapter break into cosmology and history.
9. Present the Watchers story in 1 Enoch as the clearest source world behind later angel and evil language.
10. Present the intertestamental gap and why 1 and 2 Maccabees matter for Hanukkah Pharisees Sadducees and the world Jesus enters.
11. Use John 10:22 as the short continuity proof that the Maccabean world did not disappear.
12. Chapter break into New Testament readability.
13. State that New Testament authors were reading and thinking in this wider textual world.
14. Present the line that reading the New Testament without these books is like reading a letter without knowing the conversation it was part of.
15. Close with the perspective note that history is not a threat to faith but a richer story.
16. End on a quiet culmination card.

- End with perspective and scale, not accusation.
