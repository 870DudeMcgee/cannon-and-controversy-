# 02 Production: Episode 02

- episode title: The First Bible: Two Different Lists
- episode code: ep-02_the-first-bible-two-lists
- runtime target: 8 to 14 minutes for the first documentary cut
- governing argument: The later canon split did not emerge from nowhere. Jewish communities were already living with two scriptural collections, and early Christianity inherited the larger one.
- tone priority: explanation first, controversy second

## Core Thesis

This episode should argue that the later Catholic-Protestant dispute rests on an older divergence. Before churches argued about which books belonged, Jews in Palestine and Jews in Alexandria were already reading different scriptural collections.

It should also function as the direct sequel to Episode 01: after the Temple's destruction, the surviving Pharisaic stream consolidated the Palestinian collection around the Hebrew Bible, while early Christians continued reading from the broader Greek scriptures already circulating in the Septuagint.

## Suggested Shot Order

1. Opener title on `The First Bible: Two Different Lists`.
2. Define canon as a list shaped by human decisions.
3. Bridge from Episode 01: the Pharisaic survivors at Yavneh help consolidate the Palestinian list while Christians keep reading the broader Greek collection.
4. Chapter break into the first scriptural world: Palestine.
5. Lower-context line on Hebrew and Aramaic scripture.
6. Present Torah, Nebiim, and Kethubim as the threefold structure.
7. Josephus evidence card on the 22-book count as a first-century snapshot of the Hebrew collection during the apostolic era.
8. Map or locator contrast: Palestine.
9. Chapter break into Alexandria.
10. Introduce the Septuagint as a Greek translation begun around 250 BC.
11. Show that the Septuagint includes the Hebrew Bible plus additional books and expansions.
12. Map or locator contrast: Alexandria, Egypt.
13. Comparison card: Hebrew Bible vs Septuagint.
14. Narration on early Christians reading from the larger Alexandrian collection.
15. Evidence strip on roughly 350 Old Testament quotations in the New Testament and the Septuagint-favoring pattern.
16. Tighten the claim: the early Church was reading the larger collection.
17. Chapter break into consequence.
18. Introduce the naming divide: Apocrypha vs Deuterocanonical.
19. State that the books were not added to the Catholic Bible.
20. State that they were removed from the Protestant Bible during the Reformation.
21. Final evidence card on continuity from the first century.
22. Outro source card.
