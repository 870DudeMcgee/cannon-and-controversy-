# 07 Rough Cut Checklist: Episode 02

- episode title: The First Bible: Two Different Lists
- episode code: ep-02_the-first-bible-two-lists
- purpose: turn Episode 02 into an editor-facing first-cut checklist that clearly establishes the two-scriptural-worlds framework

## Why This Episode Matters

- It explains the series-wide claim that the canon split begins before the Reformation.
- It teaches the viewer to think in terms of inherited scriptural worlds rather than one fixed early Bible.
- If this episode is unclear, every later argument about removal, inheritance, and canon criteria loses force.

## Use Order

1. Cut from `04_visual_beats.md` first.
2. Pull all approved wording from `05_assets_text.md`.
3. Use `03_script.md` only after the comparison structure is already clear.
4. Use `06_shot_bucket.csv` when a map, codex, or comparison surface needs support.

## Rough Cut Win Condition

- The viewer understands by the midpoint that there were two scriptural worlds in play.
- Josephus lands as the clearest witness for the narrower Hebrew collection.
- The Septuagint lands as the wider Greek inheritance of diaspora Judaism and early Christianity.
- The closing `removed later, not added later` claim feels inevitable rather than argumentative.

## Pass 1: Build The Skeleton

- Build the cut in the exact beat order from `04_visual_beats.md`.
- Keep the early handoff from Episode 01 brief and functional.
- Let the Hebrew witness and Septuagint world arrive as distinct chapters, not as one blended lecture.
- Simplify the visuals as the argument tightens toward the final comparison and closing line.

## Beat Checklist

### Beats 1 Through 4: Open The Question Cleanly

- Use the approved opener title from `05_assets_text.md`.
- Make the first conceptual turn land quickly: canon is a decision, not a dropped table from heaven.
- Do not treat the opening two-worlds card as the climax. It is the doorway into the episode.

### Beats 5 Through 9: Establish The Hebrew Witness

- Insert Chapter Card 01 `The Hebrew Witness` at the first strong turn into the Palestinian collection.
- Let Josephus read clearly as the key witness beat.
- Use the narrower-collection evidence card from `05_assets_text.md` as the core support layer.
- Keep labels sparse enough that the Torah, Prophets, and Writings logic stays readable in motion.

### Beats 10 Through 16: Establish The Septuagint World

- Insert Chapter Card 02 `The Septuagint World` when the episode pivots into Alexandria and Greek scripture.
- Let the Septuagint lower third and Alexandria framing do the orientation work first.
- Hold the New Testament quotation-pattern card longer than most other cards. This is the strongest explanatory proof in the episode.
- Keep the early Christian inheritance beat readable as continuity, not controversy.

### Beats 17 Through 22: Resolve The Terminology Fight

- Use the dual-column Hebrew Bible versus Septuagint comparison card as the key final visual.
- Keep the terminology turn clean: Apocrypha versus Deuterocanonical should clarify the difference in traditions, not become a taxonomy lesson.
- End on the approved closing line `Removed later, not added later` with minimal motion.

## Pass 2: Text Discipline

- Default to short labels first, then add one reinforcing line only where needed.
- Avoid stacking a map, a comparison card, and a dense lower third in the same beat.
- Let Josephus and the Septuagint speak through one strong card each rather than several medium ones.
- Use the final comparison sheet from `05_assets_text.md` as the decisive on-screen summary.

## Pass 3: Rhythm And Timing

- If the episode feels abstract early, shorten setup before Josephus rather than trimming Josephus himself.
- If the Septuagint section feels rushed, add time there before expanding the final terminology section.
- If the end feels too argumentative, strip away extra motion and let `removed later, not added later` resolve by itself.

## Do Not Do These On The First Cut

- Do not front-load the Reformation dispute.
- Do not let the opening handoff from Episode 01 become a recap episode.
- Do not overload the dual-column comparison card.
- Do not let terminology outrun the viewer's grasp of the two inherited scriptural worlds.

## Editor Sign-Off Checklist

- The viewer can explain the Hebrew witness and the Septuagint world in simple terms.
- Josephus lands as a key historical anchor.
- The New Testament quotation pattern is clearly visible.
- The final comparison clarifies inheritance before naming later removal.
- The episode closes on `removed later, not added later` without visual clutter.
