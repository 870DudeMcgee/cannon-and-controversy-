# 07 Rough Cut Checklist: Episode 05

- episode title: Referenced Inside the Bible
- episode code: ep-05_referenced-inside-the-bible
- purpose: turn Episode 05 into an editor-facing first-cut checklist that keeps the internal-biblical evidence clear and cumulative

## Why This Episode Matters

- It proves the removed texts are not merely adjacent to the Bible but active inside its language world.
- It gives the series its strongest internal-evidence episode.
- If this episode gets sensational, the argument stops sounding textual and starts sounding speculative.

## Use Order

1. Cut from `04_visual_beats.md` first.
2. Pull all approved wording from `05_assets_text.md`.
3. Use `03_script.md` only after the quotation and allusion sequence is already clear.
4. Use `06_shot_bucket.csv` only when a citation board or supporting surface needs to be simplified.

## Rough Cut Win Condition

- Jude quoting Enoch lands as the clearest proof card in the episode.
- The Assumption of Moses and Jannes/Jambres material reads as confirming evidence, not clutter.
- The allusion section feels cumulative one case at a time.
- The Jesus-Enoch finale sounds careful and text-driven rather than overstated.

## Pass 1: Build The Skeleton

- Build the cut in the exact beat order from `04_visual_beats.md`.
- Reach the direct-quotation section quickly and give it the cleanest visual discipline in the episode.
- Treat the allusion density section as ordered accumulation, not a montage dump.
- Slow slightly for the Jesus-Enoch section and the final hidden-in-plain-sight close.

## Beat Checklist

### Beats 1 Through 5: Frame The Hidden Presence

- Use the approved opener title from `05_assets_text.md`.
- Make the opening claim simple: the issue is not only what was removed, but what remains active inside the New Testament world.
- Keep the opening atmosphere restrained enough that the first direct-quotation beat feels decisive when it arrives.

### Beats 6 Through 12: Lead With Direct Quotations

- Insert Chapter Card 01 `Jude Quotes Enoch` at the turn into the clearest proof layer.
- Let the Jude 14-15 quote card sit the longest. It is the strongest proof card in the episode.
- Use the `Jude names Enoch` evidence card to make the direct-quotation claim explicit.
- Keep the Jude 9 / Assumption of Moses and Jannes/Jambres material simpler than the Enoch card. These are confirming witnesses, not the centerpiece.

### Beats 13 Through 18: Build Allusion Density Carefully

- Insert Chapter Card 02 `Allusion Density` when the episode widens beyond direct quotation.
- Run Hebrews, Wisdom, Sirach, and 2 Maccabees one case at a time.
- Use the Hebrews 1:3 and Wisdom 7:26 card as the cleanest bridge from direct citation into conceptual density.
- Keep the argument cumulative. Do not let multiple allusion cards pile up in one beat.

### Beats 19 Through Final Beat: Slow Down For Jesus And Enochic World

- Insert Chapter Card 03 `Jesus and the Enochic World` at the final interpretive turn.
- Emphasize motif clustering over line-by-line certainty.
- Use the Jesus-Enoch parallels as a carefully bounded frame, not a totalizing claim.
- End on the approved closing line `Still present in the language` with near-stillness.

## Pass 2: Text Discipline

- Let citations do the work. The episode should feel citation-led more than design-led.
- Keep the Jude card visually simplest and longest.
- Use evidence strips to support the middle cases, not to bury them.
- If a card already carries a direct quotation, do not add another dense explanatory overlay on top.

## Pass 3: Rhythm And Timing

- If the opening feels slow, shorten setup before Jude rather than trimming the Jude quote.
- If the middle feels overloaded, reduce the number of simultaneous allusion examples instead of adding transitions.
- If the Jesus-Enoch section feels too strong, simplify wording and let motif clustering carry the point.
- The close should feel like recognition, not triumph.

## Do Not Do These On The First Cut

- Do not sensationalize Enoch.
- Do not bury the Jude quotation under heavy motion or decorative design.
- Do not present every allusion as equally strong.
- Do not overstate Jesus-Enoch dependence beyond the evidence sheet's framing.

## Editor Sign-Off Checklist

- Jude 14-15 lands as the clearest proof beat.
- The Assumption of Moses and Jannes/Jambres beats support rather than distract.
- The allusion section remains one-case-at-a-time and readable.
- The Jesus-Enoch section stays careful and text-first.
- The episode closes on hidden presence rather than polemic.
