# 02 Production: Episode 05

- episode title: Referenced Inside the Bible
- episode code: ep-05_referenced-inside-the-bible
- runtime target: 10 to 16 minutes for the first documentary cut
- governing argument: The removed books are not simply absent from the Bible. They remain embedded in direct quotations, strong allusions, and the conceptual architecture of New Testament writing.
- tone priority: evidence clarity first, controversy second

## Core Thesis

This episode should argue that later canon exclusion did not remove these texts from biblical discourse itself. New Testament authors quote them, echo them, and assume their readers know them.

It should also function as the direct sequel to Episode 04: once the viewer sees that excluded texts were ancient, copied, and preserved, Episode 05 shows that those same texts continue to live inside the Bible through quotation, allusion, and motif clustering.

## Suggested Shot Order

1. Opener title on `Referenced Inside the Bible`.
2. Bridge from Episode 04: the buried texts do not stay buried; they remain inside biblical discourse.
3. State the core claim: the removed books are cited, echoed, and assumed inside the New Testament.
4. Chapter break into direct quotations.
5. Present Jude 14-15 and 1 Enoch 1:9 as the clearest direct citation.
6. Present Jude 9 and the Assumption of Moses as a second direct source case.
7. Present 2 Timothy 3:8 and Jannes and Jambres as a third direct source case.
8. Chapter break into strong allusions.
9. Show Hebrews 11 and 2 Maccabees 6-7.
10. Show Hebrews 1 and Wisdom 7:26.
11. Show John 1 with Wisdom 7-9 and Sirach 24.
12. Show James 1:19 with Sirach 5:11.
13. Show 2 Peter 2:4 and Jude 6 with Enochic angel imprisonment.
14. Show Matthew 24 and 1 Maccabees 1:54.
15. Chapter break into Jesus and 1 Enoch.
16. Introduce the claim carefully: not one phrase, but recurring Enochic judgment grammar.
17. Present delegated judgment and throne-of-glory courtroom parallels.

19. Close on the historian's caution: thematic uptake need not require line-by-line copying.
20. Final evidence card: the removed books remain hidden in plain sight inside the Bible.
21. Outro source card.
