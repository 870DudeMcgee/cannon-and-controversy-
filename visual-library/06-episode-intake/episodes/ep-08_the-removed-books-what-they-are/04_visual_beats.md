# 04 Visual Beats: Episode 08

- episode title: The Removed Books: What They Are
- episode code: ep-08_the-removed-books-what-they-are
- use with: `02_production.md`, `05_assets_text.md`, and `06_shot_bucket.csv`
- purpose: first assembly guide for narration beats, screen text, and visual order

## Editorial Rule

Every beat should make the removed books concrete. Keep the episode profile-driven, legible, and visually disciplined so the viewer remembers the books as specific works rather than as one blurred category.

## Act 1: Excluded, Not Lost

### Beat 1

- visual:
	- cold open title plate with codex texture and minimal ornament
- narration beat:
	- Open by asking what the removed books actually are.
- screen text:
	- The Removed Books: What They Are
- notes:
	- Hold just long enough to orient the episode.

### Beat 2

- visual:
	- opener correction card with clean canonical contrast
- narration beat:
	- State that these books are not lost and are still printed in Catholic and Orthodox Bibles.
- screen text:
	- Excluded, not lost
- notes:
	- This is the thesis lock for the whole episode.

### Beat 3

- visual:
	- continuity card from early Christian Old Testament to the Reformation split
- narration beat:
	- Explain that these are concrete books later excluded from Protestant Bibles beginning with Luther.
- screen text:
	- Present for centuries
- notes:
	- Keep the claim concise.

## Assembly Notes

- Beats 1 through 3 should quickly undo the `lost books` misconception.
- Beats 5 and 6 should keep narrative books and history books distinct.
- Beats 8 and 9 should sound literary and concrete rather than abstractly theological.
- Beats 10 through 12 should make shorter Protestant Esther and Daniel visually obvious without overcrowding the screen.
