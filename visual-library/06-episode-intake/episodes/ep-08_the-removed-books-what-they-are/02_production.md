# 02 Production: Episode 08

- episode title: The Removed Books: What They Are
- episode code: ep-08_the-removed-books-what-they-are
- runtime target: 10 to 15 minutes for the first documentary cut
- governing argument: The removed books are specific texts with distinct genres, dates, settings, and theological contributions. They are not lost, and they were not a vague medieval add-on. They were concrete books later excluded from Protestant Bibles.
- tone priority: clarity and orientation first, controversy second

## Core Thesis

This episode should argue that readers need to know what these books actually are before later arguments about doctrine or canon can be understood clearly. Each book carries a different function: story, history, wisdom, repentance, martyrdom, resurrection, and anti-idolatry argument.

It should function as a companion-profile episode after Episode 07: once viewers understand why the canon debate matters, Episode 08 lets them see the actual books that were left outside one tradition's Bible.

## Suggested Shot Order

1. Open on the claim that the removed books are not lost.
2. State that they remained in Catholic and Orthodox Bibles and were excluded from Protestant Bibles beginning with Luther.
3. Chapter break into the seven-book survey.
4. Present Tobit as the angel-prayer-almsgiving story.
5. Present Judith as faith-under-siege and female-heroism theology.
6. Present 1 Maccabees as the historical bridge and Hanukkah origin text.
7. Present 2 Maccabees as the martyrdom-resurrection-prayers-for-the-dead text.
8. Chapter break into wisdom and exile books.
9. Present Wisdom of Solomon as immortality, divine Wisdom, and anti-idolatry argument.
10. Present Sirach as practical wisdom and a major New Testament source world for James.
11. Present Baruch as repentance, exile, and a text historically attached to Jeremiah.
12. Chapter break into additions to Esther and Daniel.
13. Show that Esther is longer and more overtly theological in Greek form.
14. Show the Song of the Three Young Men, Susanna, and Bel and the Dragon as missing Daniel material.
15. Close on the claim that these books were excluded from one canon tradition, not lost to history.
