# 07 Rough Cut Checklist: Episode 08

- episode title: The Removed Books: What They Are
- episode code: ep-08_the-removed-books-what-they-are
- purpose: turn Episode 08 into the first assembly template for the full nine-episode series

## Why This Is the Template Episode

- It is card-rich without being timeline-heavy.
- It teaches the series rhythm clearly: opener, correction, chapter break, profile cards, comparison turn, closing thesis.
- It is easier to pace than Episode 09 because it does not need dense date stacking to work.
- If this episode cuts cleanly, the same card cadence can be reused across the rest of the series.

## Use Order

1. Cut from `04_visual_beats.md` first.
2. Pull all wording from `05_assets_text.md`.
3. Use `03_script.md` only to tighten narration timing and emphasis.
4. Use `06_shot_bucket.csv` only when you need to source or swap supporting visuals.

## Rough Cut Win Condition

- A first-time viewer can name at least four of the removed books after one watch.
- The viewer understands that these books were excluded from one canon tradition, not lost.
- The Esther and Daniel additions read as shorter-versus-longer book forms, not as an abstract note.
- No single card overstays long enough to make the episode feel like a catalog slideshow.

## Pass 1: Build The Skeleton

- Build the episode in the exact Beat 1 through Beat 12 order from `04_visual_beats.md`.
- Do not invent alternate titles, extra cards, or denser comparison boards in this pass.
- Keep motion simple: cut, dissolve, slight push, or restrained texture drift.
- Do not stop to polish atmosphere while the structure is still unproven.

## Beat Checklist

### Beats 1 Through 3: Correct The Misunderstanding Fast

- Beat 1:
  - Place the opener title `The Removed Books: What They Are`.
  - Use the supporting line from `05_assets_text.md`: `Excluded from one canon tradition, not lost to history` if the opener needs a second line.
- Beat 2:
  - Drop Quote Card 01 `Excluded, not lost`.
  - Keep this card long enough to reset the viewer's assumption, then move on.
- Beat 3:
  - Place Evidence Card 01 `Seven books, not a vague appendix`.
  - Make sure this lands before the profiles begin so the category is concrete.

### Beats 4 Through 6: Establish Story And History

- Beat 4:
  - Insert Chapter Card 01 `Story and History`.
- Beat 5:
  - Run Tobit and Judith as separate profile turns, not as one merged block.
  - Use safest lower thirds first unless the stronger documentary variant reads better in motion.
- Beat 6:
  - Use the paired Maccabean comparison structure.
  - Place Evidence Card 02 `The Maccabean pair`.
  - Drop Quote Card 02 from 2 Maccabees 12 only if it sharpens the turn instead of slowing it.

### Beats 7 Through 9: Widen The World

- Beat 7:
  - Insert Chapter Card 02 `Wisdom and Exile`.
- Beat 8:
  - Run Wisdom of Solomon and Sirach as distinct cards with different emphasis.
  - Keep the James echo visible in the Sirach turn.
  - Use Quote Card 03 `Wisdom behind later Christology` if the Wisdom beat needs theological lift.
- Beat 9:
  - Keep Baruch compact and calm.
  - Use the `Attached to Jeremiah in ancient use` line only if it improves orientation.

### Beats 10 Through 12: Make The Shorter Books Obvious

- Beat 10:
  - Insert the chapter break into additions to existing books.
  - Make `Shorter Esther` and `Shorter Daniel` immediately readable.
- Beat 11:
  - Use the Esther and Daniel additions comparison card.
  - Support this with Evidence Card 03 `Esther and Daniel are longer in Greek`.
  - Avoid tables unless the simple side-by-side version fails.
- Beat 12:
  - End on the safest end-card version:
  - `These books were excluded from one canon tradition.`
  - `They were not erased from history.`
  - Leave space for a slow fade and stillness.

## Pass 2: Text Discipline

- Default to the safest lower-third version on the first cut.
- Upgrade to stronger documentary variants only where there is enough screen time to read them cleanly.
- Keep source strips short and use them only when they add orientation.
- If a beat already has a chapter card or evidence card, avoid stacking another dense text layer on top of it.

## Pass 3: Rhythm And Timing

- If the episode drags, shorten Beat 5 before cutting Beat 6 or Beat 8.
- If the episode feels too list-like, add half a beat of breathing room after Beat 6 and Beat 9 rather than adding new cards.
- If the additions section feels abrupt, slow the transition into Beat 10 instead of rewriting Beat 11.
- Protect the final line. The close should feel settled, not argumentative.

## Do Not Do These On The First Cut

- Do not add extra title variants.
- Do not overbuild geography cards unless the viewer is getting lost.
- Do not turn the Esther and Daniel material into a dense chart.
- Do not let 2 Maccabees dominate the whole episode just because it carries later doctrinal weight.

## Reuse Pattern For The Rest Of The Series

- Episodes with heavy argument beats should reuse the Episode 08 structure: correction, chapter break, focused card run, category reset, quiet close.
- Episode 09 should borrow the same restraint, but with fewer cards per beat because the chronology load is higher.
- When in doubt, use one strong card per beat instead of two medium cards.

## Editor Sign-Off Checklist

- The opener corrects the `lost books` misconception within the first three beats.
- The seven-book set is clearly named before the middle of the episode.
- 1 Maccabees and 2 Maccabees are distinguished clearly.
- Wisdom, Sirach, and Baruch each get a distinct identity.
- The additions to Esther and Daniel are visually obvious.
- The final line lands as orientation, not polemic.
