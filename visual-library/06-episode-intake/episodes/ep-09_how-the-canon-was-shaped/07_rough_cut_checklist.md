# 07 Rough Cut Checklist: Episode 09

- episode title: How the Canon Was Shaped
- episode code: ep-09_how-the-canon-was-shaped
- purpose: turn Episode 09 into an editor-facing first-cut checklist that makes the final canon chronology legible on first viewing

## Why This Episode Matters

- It is the series finale and has to convert a long chronology into something viewers can retain.
- It carries the highest density of dates, names, and institutions in the series.
- If this episode gets crowded, the viewer will remember controversy but not sequence.

## Use Order

1. Cut from `04_visual_beats.md` first.
2. Pull all approved wording from `05_assets_text.md`.
3. Use `03_script.md` only after the chronology spine is readable on its own.
4. Use `06_shot_bucket.csv` only when a milestone, witness, or transition surface needs visual support.

## Rough Cut Win Condition

- The viewer leaves knowing the canon was shaped across centuries, not fixed in one moment.
- The early divergence between Septuagint inheritance and post-70 rabbinic narrowing is understandable.
- Witness, council, translation, Reformation, and print-distribution stages feel distinct.
- The closing line lands on centuries of shaping normalized by print.

## Pass 1: Build The Skeleton

- Build the cut in the exact beat order from `04_visual_beats.md`.
- Keep chronology first in every act.
- Use fewer cards per beat than Episode 08 because the information load is higher.
- Simplify rather than decorate whenever dates and names begin to stack.

## Beat Checklist

### Beats 1 Through 3: Establish The Early Divergence

- Use the approved opener title `How the Canon Was Shaped`.
- Insert Chapter Card 01 `Septuagint and Yavneh` early so the viewer has the right categories from the start.
- Use Evidence Card 01 `The early divergence` as the opening explanatory anchor.
- Keep the Yavneh or Jamnia beat nuanced: process, not one neat council.

### Beats 4 Through 6: Move Into Witness And Uncertainty

- Insert Chapter Card 02 `Witness and Uncertainty` at the shift into early Christian practice.
- Let Irenaeus and Origen read one witness at a time.
- Keep Athanasius as a clarifying second-tier beat rather than an overloaded side note.
- Use the middle-cluster summary card `From witness to decision` only after the individual witnesses have landed.

### Beats 7 Through 9: Show Formalization Without Oversimplifying

- Use the Rome, Hippo, Carthage, and Jerome cluster to show formalization plus unresolved doubt.
- Keep the council dates on the spine or source strips rather than in oversized text blocks.
- Let Jerome function as the hinge into later doubt and translation influence.

### Beats 10 Through 12: Narrow Into Demotion, Exclusion, And Print Absence

- Use the Reformation sequence with disciplined spacing: Luther, Trent, Westminster, Bible Society.
- Place Luther's preface quote before Westminster's exclusion quote so the demotion-to-hardening sequence is visible.
- Use Evidence Card 03 `Luther to the print cutoff` as the final explanatory summary.
- End on Quote Card 03 and the approved closing line `The Bible list was shaped by centuries, then normalized by print.`

## Pass 2: Text Discipline

- Use the safest lower thirds first, especially for named figures and institutions.
- Do not stack more than one major milestone card and one identifying strip in a single beat.
- Keep the chronology spine readable at all times.
- Let the closing synthesis card do more of the interpretive work than extra overlays.

## Pass 3: Rhythm And Timing

- If the opening feels crowded, shorten phrasing before cutting the early-divergence logic.
- If the witness section blurs together, slow the transitions between Irenaeus, Origen, and Athanasius.
- If the Reformation sequence feels rushed, simplify side detail before extending Westminster.
- The finale should feel increasingly clear as it moves forward, not increasingly dense.

## Do Not Do These On The First Cut

- Do not overload one beat with too many dates.
- Do not treat Yavneh, Trent, or Westminster as if any one alone explains the whole story.
- Do not merge witness and decision stages into one flat chronology.
- Do not end on polemic when the approved ending is historical synthesis.

## Editor Sign-Off Checklist

- The early divergence is understandable on first viewing.
- Witness, decision, translation, Reformation, and print-cutoff stages are distinct.
- Luther's demotion clearly precedes Westminster's formal exclusion.
- The 1826 print cutoff lands as the practical reason most Protestants never saw the books.
- The finale closes on a chain of decisions across centuries rather than one decisive moment.
