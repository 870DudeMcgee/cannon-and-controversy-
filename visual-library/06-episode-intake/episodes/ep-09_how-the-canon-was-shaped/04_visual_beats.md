## Editorial Rule

Every beat should preserve chronology first. The viewer should leave understanding sequence, not just controversy. Use dates, names, and transitions to create a readable chain of decisions.

- Beats 2 and 3 should establish the early timeline without overexplaining Jamnia.

- Beats 8 and 9 should feel like standardization with one important internal crack: Jerome.
- Beats 11 and 12 should show that common Protestant absence is a later print-cultural result, not just a sixteenth-century switch.
