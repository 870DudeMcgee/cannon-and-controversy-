# 07 Rough Cut Checklist: Episode 04

- episode title: The Deeper Removed Texts
- episode code: ep-04_the-deeper-removed-texts
- purpose: turn Episode 04 into an editor-facing first-cut checklist that keeps the buried-text argument evidentiary and calm

## Why This Episode Matters

- It widens the series beyond the deuterocanonical books into the deeper textual ecosystem.
- It gives the viewer hard physical evidence through Qumran and the Dead Sea Scrolls.
- If this episode becomes mystical or treasure-hunt styled, the larger canon argument weakens immediately.

## Use Order

1. Cut from `04_visual_beats.md` first.
2. Pull all approved wording from `05_assets_text.md`.
3. Use `03_script.md` only after the physical-evidence chain is already clear.
4. Use `06_shot_bucket.csv` when cave, fragment, or archive visuals need to be sourced or simplified.

## Rough Cut Win Condition

- The viewer understands that excluded later does not mean insignificant earlier.
- Qumran reads as evidence of preservation under pressure, not as a mystery site.
- The cave contrast is legible before the disaster-recovery interpretation appears.
- 1 Enoch lands as the clearest deeper canon problem in the episode.

## Pass 1: Build The Skeleton

- Build the cut in the exact beat order from `04_visual_beats.md`.
- Keep the first act concise enough that the episode reaches the caves quickly.
- Let site evidence lead, then consequence, then Enoch.
- Reduce ornament as the final exclusion argument tightens.

## Beat Checklist

### Beats 1 Through 4: Open Beyond The Official List

- Use the approved opener title from `05_assets_text.md`.
- Establish early that the issue is not only books removed from Protestant Bibles.
- Keep the opening language focused on circulation, reverence, and preserved usage.

### Beats 5 Through 11: Show Qumran Under Pressure

- Insert Chapter Card 01 `Qumran Under Pressure` at the shift into site evidence.
- Use the `Qumran Under Pressure` evidence card early in the cave section.
- Make the Cave 4 versus Caves 1 and 11 contrast visually obvious before any interpretation card appears.
- Let the disaster-recovery line arrive only after the viewer has seen the physical pattern.

### Beats 12 Through 16: Explain What The Scrolls Prove

- Use the explanatory cards from `05_assets_text.md` to move from site facts into canon consequences.
- Keep this section analytical rather than atmospheric.
- Make diversity of textual use visible without turning the sequence into a dense taxonomy lecture.

### Beats 17 Through Final Beat: Isolate Enoch And Close Quietly

- Insert Chapter Card 02 `1 Enoch Preserved` when the episode narrows to the clearest case study.
- Slow down slightly here. Enoch is the conceptual center of the episode.
- Use the `1 Enoch at Qumran` evidence card and the `This was not a fringe text` correction line as the anchor.
- End on the approved closing line `Excluded later. Preserved earlier.` with almost no ornament.

## Pass 2: Text Discipline

- Lead with site, cave, fragment, and preservation language before interpretation.
- Keep Enoch labels simple and ancient-looking, not mystical.
- Avoid stacking a locator, cave evidence card, and quote card all at once.
- Let the closing exclusion card work through stillness rather than emphasis.

## Pass 3: Rhythm And Timing

- If the episode drags early, shorten setup before the cave evidence.
- If the cave sequence feels confusing, simplify the contrast rather than adding more labels.
- If the ending feels too sudden, add space around the Enoch sequence, not around the opener.
- The episode should feel more revelatory as it goes, but never sensational.

## Do Not Do These On The First Cut

- Do not use mystical or occult framing.
- Do not introduce disaster-recovery interpretation before the physical cave evidence is shown.
- Do not let atmosphere overwhelm the explanatory center of the episode.
- Do not decorate the final exclusion line.

## Editor Sign-Off Checklist

- Qumran reads as a preservation story under Roman pressure.
- Cave 4 versus Caves 1 and 11 is clearly legible.
- The viewer understands what the Scrolls prove about earlier textual diversity.
- 1 Enoch lands as ancient, copied, and preserved rather than fringe.
- The final line closes the deeper canon problem quietly.
