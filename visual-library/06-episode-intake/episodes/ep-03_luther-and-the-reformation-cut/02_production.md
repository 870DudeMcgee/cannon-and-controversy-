# 02 Production: Episode 03

- episode title: The Reformation Cut: Luther and the Protestant Bible
- episode code: ep-03_luther-and-the-reformation-cut
- runtime target: 9 to 15 minutes for the first documentary cut
- governing argument: The disputed books did not disappear by accident. In the Reformation, Luther and later Protestant tradition turned inherited canon tension into a doctrinally motivated cut.
- tone priority: documentary clarity first, controversy second

## Core Thesis

This episode should argue that the Protestant Old Testament did not emerge by simply recovering the original Christian Bible. It emerged through a Reformation-era decision to exclude books that conflicted with a specific theological program.

It should also function as the direct sequel to Episode 02: once the viewer understands that early Christians inherited the larger Alexandrian collection, Episode 03 shows the historical moment that collection is explicitly downgraded and then cut within Protestant tradition.

## Suggested Shot Order

1. Opener title on `The Reformation Cut`.
2. Bridge from Episode 02: the larger Christian scriptural world now meets a Reformation decision point.
3. State that the removal was not primarily scholarly but theological.
4. Introduce Luther's `sola fide` conflict and the practices tied to it.
5. Chapter break into Leipzig and 2 Maccabees.
6. Present 2 Maccabees 12:44-46 as a pressure point in the debate over Purgatory and prayers for the dead.
7. Introduce Johann Eck as the opponent using the text at Leipzig in 1519.
8. State Luther's move clearly: he does not dissolve the verse; he denies the book's scriptural status.
9. Luther preface quote card: `profitable and good to read`.
10. Tighten the consequence: this is a reversal of earlier Christian authorities and councils.
11. Chapter break into canon criteria.
12. Present the three criteria for canonicity.
13. Show the first criterion eliminating the deuterocanonical books.
14. Show the third criterion nearly eliminating James.
15. James `epistle of straw` evidence card.
16. Widen the claim: Luther's logic destabilizes more than the Old Testament margin.
17. Chapter break into Protestant hardening.
18. Move from Luther to Calvin and then Westminster.
19. Contrast Luther's `profitable and good to read` line with Westminster's non-scripture position.
20. Final evidence card: the Reformation cut is hardened.
21. Outro source card.
