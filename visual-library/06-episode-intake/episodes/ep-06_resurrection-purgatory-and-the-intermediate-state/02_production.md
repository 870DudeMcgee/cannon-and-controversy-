# 02 Production: Episode 06

- episode title: Resurrection, Purgatory, and the Intermediate State
- episode code: ep-06_resurrection-purgatory-and-the-intermediate-state
- runtime target: 10 to 16 minutes for the first documentary cut
- governing argument: Once deuterocanonical books are removed, the afterlife debate has to be rebuilt from a thinner textual base. Episode 06 shows how the intermediate state and final resurrection remain in view while explicit purification texts become disputed or unavailable.
- tone priority: clarification first and controversy second

## Core Thesis

This episode should argue that the afterlife debate is not only about theology. It is also about source authority. The same canon decisions traced in the earlier episodes directly shape how traditions explain post-death consciousness, purification, and final resurrection.

It should function as the direct sequel to Episode 05: once viewers see that disputed books remain active inside biblical discourse, Episode 06 shows what changes when later traditions let some of those voices count and silence others.

## Suggested Shot Order

1. Open by carrying Episode 05's hidden-source argument into doctrine consequences.
2. State the central question: why final resurrection still matters if many traditions already affirm conscious existence after death.
3. Introduce the two-stage model: intermediate state and final resurrection.
4. Present the high-level comparison by tradition.
5. Frame the problem as a source-authority question, not only a doctrine dispute.
6. Chapter break into Luke 16.
7. Present Luke 16 as the clearest New Testament picture of post-death consciousness before final resurrection.
8. Show the features of Luke 16: conscious awareness, distinct conditions, the great gulf, and moral continuity.
9. State the caution: Luke 16 alone does not settle later technical purgatory.
10. Show why Luke 16 still anchors intermediate-state debates across traditions.
11. Chapter break into 1 Enoch 22.
12. Present 1 Enoch 22 as a structured realm-of-the-dead model with separated compartments.
13. Show how Enoch 22 clarifies Luke 16's logic of separation and deferred final judgment.
14. State the historical implication: New Testament afterlife language sits inside a Second Temple conceptual world, not a flat Sheol model.
15. Chapter break into canon consequences.
16. Present 2 Maccabees 12:44-46 as the clearest prayers-for-the-dead and purification text.
17. Present Wisdom 3:1-4 as the clearest immortality-of-the-soul text and hold it beside the resurrection passages that remain accepted.
18. Show the three-step methodological shift: remove the explicit purification text, keep the broader afterlife tension, then build a narrower model from the remaining canon.
19. Clarify that this is not necessarily bad faith; it is a source-authority change with doctrinal consequences.
20. Close on the thesis that canon choices shape doctrine outcomes.

## Editing Rules

- Keep the distinction between intermediate state and final resurrection visible at every stage.
- Do not claim that Luke 16 or 1 Enoch 22 by themselves settle every later doctrinal formulation.
- Let the canon consequence emerge from the evidence instead of sounding like sectarian attack.
- Keep continuity explicit with Episode 05 in the opener and with Episode 07-level stakes in the close.
