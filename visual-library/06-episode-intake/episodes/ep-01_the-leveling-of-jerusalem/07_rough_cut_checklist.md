# 07 Rough Cut Checklist: Episode 01

- episode title: The Leveling of Jerusalem
- episode code: ep-01_the-leveling-of-jerusalem
- purpose: turn Episode 01 into an editor-facing first-cut checklist that can launch the series cleanly

## Why This Episode Matters

- It establishes the whole series tone: political, historical, and cumulative rather than devotional.
- It teaches the viewer how the documentary moves from event, to pressure, to collapse, to canon consequence.
- If Episode 01 drifts into spectacle or sentimentality, the rest of the series will inherit the wrong rhythm.

## Use Order

1. Cut from `04_visual_beats.md` first.
2. Pull all approved wording from `05_assets_text.md`.
3. Use `03_script.md` to tighten narration timing only after the skeleton is working.
4. Use `06_shot_bucket.csv` only when a supporting visual needs to be sourced or swapped.

## Rough Cut Win Condition

- The viewer understands within the first act that this is a political-pressure story, not a passion-play recap.
- Caiaphas, Josephus, James, and Yavneh all land as distinct authority beats.
- Jerusalem's fall feels like the hinge into canon fracture, not the end of a war documentary.
- The closing line lands on canon consequence, not on ruin imagery alone.

## Pass 1: Build The Skeleton

- Build the cut in the exact beat order from `04_visual_beats.md`.
- Keep the first pass visually restrained: maps, witness cards, timeline spine, pressure montage, then aftermath.
- Do not add ornamental crucifixion imagery or extra spectacle transitions.
- Protect the move from public execution to social fracture to post-Temple consolidation.

## Beat Checklist

### Beats 1 Through 8: Set Pressure, Not Piety

- Beat 1:
  - Use the approved opener title from `05_assets_text.md`.
  - Keep the supporting line focused on the forty-year fuse.
- Beats 2 through 4:
  - Keep Jerusalem and the timeline marker simple and immediate.
  - Do not overcut before the viewer understands place and timeframe.
- Beats 5 through 7:
  - Use Chapter Card 01 `Pressure Before the Fire` at the first clean turn.
  - Introduce Caiaphas with the safest lower third first.
  - Let the John 11:49-50 witness card read clearly before moving on.
- Beat 8:
  - Make `A crisis begins` feel like the ignition point for the whole episode.

### Beats 9 Through 15: Show The Fire Spreading

- Beat 9:
  - Use the chapter-break beat as a reset into widening pressure.
- Beats 10 through 12:
  - Keep the Claudius and Suetonius evidence moving.
  - This is proof of expansion, not a history detour.
- Beats 13 through 15:
  - Let the geography widen, then bring Josephus in as the stabilizing historical witness.
  - Avoid overlabeling the unrest montage.

### Beats 16 Through The Temple-Fall Turn: Remove The Bridge

- Beat 16:
  - Insert Chapter Card 02 `The Death That Cracked the Dam`.
- James sequence:
  - Let James feel quiet and human before the harder Ananus turn.
  - Use the safest James lower third and Josephus-linked source strip if the beat needs authority.
- Ananus sequence:
  - Keep the illegal-execution framing procedural.
  - Do not lean into accusation language.
- Temple-fall turn:
  - Let the collapse feel like a structural break, not only a visual climax.

### Final Yavneh And Ruins Sequence

- Post-collapse section:
  - Insert Chapter Card 03 `Yavneh After the Ash` when the episode moves from destruction to consolidation.
  - Use the Yavneh lower third as the transition into canon consequence.
- Final beat:
  - End on the approved closing line `The split begins in the ruins.`
  - Leave enough stillness that the viewer feels the historical hinge.

## Pass 2: Text Discipline

- Default to the safest lower thirds first.
- Add source strips only where they strengthen a witness beat.
- If a beat already has a chapter card or witness card, do not stack another dense text layer on top of it.
- Keep pressure language sober and readable in under two seconds whenever possible.

## Pass 3: Rhythm And Timing

- If the opening feels slow, shorten Beat 2 before trimming Caiaphas or Josephus.
- If the middle feels muddy, simplify the unrest montage rather than adding more labels.
- If the Temple-fall section starts to dominate, shorten spectacle and preserve the Yavneh consequence turn.
- The end should feel like history hardening into a canon problem.

## Do Not Do These On The First Cut

- Do not front-load devotional crucifixion imagery.
- Do not turn Josephus and Suetonius into long citation lectures.
- Do not let the Temple destruction swallow the James and Yavneh beats.
- Do not end on ruins alone without the canon consequence line.

## Editor Sign-Off Checklist

- The first act reads as political pressure, not religious melodrama.
- Caiaphas, Claudius, Josephus, James, and Yavneh all land distinctly.
- The widening unrest is legible before the Temple falls.
- The James execution functions as a hinge, not a side note.
- The episode closes on canon fracture beginning in the ruins.
