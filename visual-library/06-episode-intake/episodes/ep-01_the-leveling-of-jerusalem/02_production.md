# 02 Production: Episode 01

- episode title: The Leveling of Jerusalem
- episode code: ep-01_the-leveling-of-jerusalem
- runtime target: 10 to 16 minutes for a first documentary cut
- governing argument: The crucifixion did not end a messianic crisis. It lit a forty-year fuse that burned through Jerusalem, the Temple, and finally into the scriptural boundary-making that shaped the canon.
- tone priority: political pressure first, theology second

## Core Thesis

This episode should argue that the destruction of Jerusalem and the Temple was not background scenery to early Christianity. It was the historical rupture that forced Judaism and Christianity to rebuild on rival scriptural terms.

## Act Structure

### Act 1: The Fuse Is Lit

Narrative job:
Establish the crucifixion as a Roman political execution, not merely a devotional image, and frame the entire episode as a forty-year chain reaction.

Core source material:
- `The Crucifixion and the Road to 70 AD`
- `"King of the Jews" — What Rome Was Really Saying`
- fact bar with ~30 AD, 49 AD, 62 AD, 40 years, and 3 direct lines

Visual approach:
- solemn opener plate
- codex/parchment reveal
- immediate Jerusalem locator
- timeline marker locked to ~30 AD

Primary buckets:
- `01-opener`

### Act 2: The Fire Spreads

Narrative job:
Show that the Jesus movement grew, fractured synagogues across the empire, and was already generating imperial-level disturbance before the war in Judea fully erupted.

Core source material:
- `The Fire That Would Not Die`
- `The Claudius Expulsion — 49 AD`
- `The Messianic Pressure Cooker: Judea, 30–66 AD`

Visual approach:
- widening map geography from Jerusalem outward
- pressure-cooker montage: Rome, synagogues, occupation, crowd unrest
- lower thirds for Claudius and diaspora fracture context

Primary buckets:
- `02-historical-context`
- `04-map-sequence`
- `06-transition-atmosphere`

### Act 3: The Bridge Is Removed

Narrative job:
Center James as the stabilizing bridge whose death accelerates collapse and strips away the moderate center.

Core source material:
- `The Death That Cracked the Dam: James, 62 AD`
- the paragraphs on James as bridge figure and Ananus's illegal execution

Visual approach:
- witness-card pacing
- chapter-break plate into a tighter, more intimate section
- restrained, human rather than spectacle-driven visuals

Primary buckets:
- `03-quote-witness`
- `07-chapter-breaks`
- `06-transition-atmosphere`

### Act 4: Jerusalem Falls, The Canon Hardens

Narrative job:
Move from the destruction of the Temple into the aftermath: Sadducees vanish, Pharisees survive, Yavneh/Jamnia consolidation follows, and the Bible split is born through process.

Core source material:
- `The Bitter Irony of History`
- `Three Lines From the Cross to the Canon`
- `The Forty-Year Chain: From the Cross to the Canon`

Visual approach:
- ash and ruin imagery
- timeline-spine escalation to 70 AD and ~85–100 AD
- end on evidence card rather than sentiment

Primary buckets:
- `05-canon-conflict`
- `06-transition-atmosphere`
- `08-outro`
